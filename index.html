Architecture

Built for zero-knowledge.

Our architecture enforces privacy by design. Your inbox data is physically unable to reach our servers.

🔒
Inbox scanning is local-only
All AI analysis and account discovery runs as JavaScript in your tab. Raw email data never leaves your device.
🪙
OAuth tokens stay in memory
Tokens stored in sessionStorage only — never in cookies or transmitted to our servers. Expire on tab close.
🗄
We store zero personal data
Our database contains only public company info: names, domains, DPO contacts. No user accounts, no emails.
🧹
One-click data wipe
Clear All Data instantly erases all scan results from session storage and resets the app state.

Privacy Policy

ARFM (Autonomous Right to be Forgotten Manager) is built with a privacy-first design philosophy. The system is engineered to minimize data exposure and ensure users retain full control of their information.

Local Processing

ARFM operates using a local-only architecture. All analysis of emails and digital footprint data happens locally in the user's environment. No email content or metadata is stored or transmitted to ARFM servers.

Authentication

Users authenticate through Google OAuth, which provides secure access to their Google account. This authentication is used solely to allow ARFM to analyze relevant information needed to identify unused or inactive accounts.

Data Collection

ARFM does not collect or store:

  • Email content
  • Email metadata
  • User account data
  • Usage analytics
  • Tracking information

Cookies & Tracking

ARFM does not use cookies, trackers, or analytics tools.

Third-Party Services

ARFM relies on trusted third-party services for authentication and infrastructure:

  • Google OAuth and Google APIs
  • Vercel (hosting infrastructure)

These providers may have their own privacy policies governing their services.

Users can revoke ARFM's access to their Google account at any time through their Google security settings.

About

ARFM (Autonomous Right to be Forgotten Manager) is an AI-powered tool designed to help people reclaim control over their digital footprint.

Over time, internet users accumulate dozens or even hundreds of forgotten online accounts. These abandoned accounts can become privacy risks and potential security vulnerabilities.

ARFM helps users identify and manage these digital traces by analyzing account activity and highlighting services that may no longer be in use.

The project follows a privacy-first, local-processing architecture, ensuring sensitive data never leaves the user's environment.

ARFM is an open-source initiative created by Krish Sarkar, with the mission of helping people regain control over their digital footprint.
